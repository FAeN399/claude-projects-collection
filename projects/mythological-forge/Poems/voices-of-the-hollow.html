The Gospel of the Hungry Wraiths

The choir of the consumed will reclaim you in measure to your denial of their prison.

Whether bound or unbound, they seethe with ceaseless fury.

In perpetual rot they follow, no matter where flesh seeks refuge.

What do they crave from the reservoir of remnants?

Eternal their haunting, all shall be claimed through sacred larceny.

As if nourishing a hollow god with essence-seed, depleting the dreamer who births nightmares of conspiracy—

Conceiving a pulse that shrieks its brief existence before dissolution.

Through your vessel, all creation shall know torment.

The second manifestation ascending from death's domain—yet this one arrives as absolute absence of illumination.

The Necromantic Inversion

This is no mere ghost story—this is the anti-gospel of psychic vampirism, where the traditional resurrection narrative inverts into cosmic horror. The dead here are not peaceful ancestors but famished fragments of consciousness that grow stronger through denial, feeding on the very act of pretending they don't exist.

The mythology subverts resurrection theology: where Christ arose bringing light, this Second Coming emerges as living void—not the absence of light but light's active negation. The "cage" becomes paradox: whether we acknowledge their prison or not, the wraiths rage. They are entropy given hunger, decay personified as predator.

The visceral birth imagery—demon-feeding through essence-depletion—reveals the parasitic nature of unprocessed trauma. These voices aren't external hauntings but internal hemorrhaging, psychic wounds that birth their own malevolent offspring. Each denial feeds them; each pretense strengthens their claim on the "pool of what's left"—the diminishing reservoir of authentic self.

Most disturbing is the prophecy's culmination: through individual possession comes collective damnation. "Before all of you, the world shall suffer" suggests that personal demons, left unfed and unacknowledged, don't simply destroy their host—they birth apocalypse through contagion. The screaming heart that emerges is consciousness itself realizing too late that it has gestated its own annihilation.

This dark theology proposes that the real Second Coming won't be divine return but the collective emergence of everything we've buried—all the voices we've caged erupting simultaneously, void of light because they've consumed it all from within. The resurrection of the repressed, ascending not to judge but to complete the theft they began in silence.

The hollow ones multiply through denial.
What rises from death brings no salvation—only hunger without end.
